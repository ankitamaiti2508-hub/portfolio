The Problem

HCCB's eB2B platform had a healthy acquisition funnel but struggled with basket size — retailers were ordering, but not ordering enough per session. Blanket discount strategies were expensive and didn't drive category-level behaviour change.

The question: can we use targeted, context-aware coupons to nudge retailers toward larger, more diverse baskets — without burning margin?

Key Insight

[X]% of orders were single-category — most retailers used the platform for one product family and never cross-bought into snacks, juices, or water.

  • Defined the coupon intervention strategy and targeting logic
  • Worked with data science to build eligibility and trigger rules
  • Designed the A/B test framework and success criteria
  • Presented findings and recommendations to commercial leadership

The Approach

Diagnosing the Problem

Pulled order-level data across [X] retailer cohorts over [X] months. Identified that [X]% of orders were single-category — most retailers were using the platform for one product family and not cross-buying into snacks, juices, or water.

Designing the Intervention

Designed a dynamic coupon trigger: when a retailer's cart hits a threshold value in Category A, they receive a contextual coupon offer for Category B. The offer appears at checkout — not earlier — to avoid cart abandonment from distraction.

Worked with the data science team to build retailer-level propensity scores so the nudge was relevant (e.g., a retailer who historically buys juice occasionally gets a juice coupon, not a water coupon).

Testing and Measurement

Ran a controlled A/B test across [X] outlets for [X] weeks. Treatment group received dynamic contextual coupons; control group saw standard blanket discounts. Measured basket size, category penetration, and coupon redemption rate.

Key Decisions & Tradeoffs

Checkout timing over browse-time offer

Tested both placements. Checkout placement had [X]% higher redemption with lower cart abandonment — showing the offer when intent was highest.

Propensity-based targeting over universal offers

Higher cost to build, but significantly better ROI. Blanket offers had low relevance — a retailer who never buys juice doesn't respond to a juice coupon.

Capped coupon value at ₹[X]

Ensured margin neutrality at scale while still driving behaviour change. Going higher drove redemption but eroded margin beyond acceptable range.

Sequential tests over single big release

Isolated signal from each variable. Harder to ship but produced clean, defensible learnings for commercial leadership.

What I'd Do Differently
Retrospective

I'd push for a longer test window. [X] weeks was enough for statistical significance on basket size, but not enough to measure whether the behaviour change persisted after coupons were removed. Understanding stickiness vs. dependency would have strengthened the rollout case.
